# Brief of Events Organised by CodeChef ZHCET Chapter in Academic Year 2025-26

**Faculty Advisor:** Professor Saiful Islam

## Session Overview

During the Academic Year 2025-26, the CodeChef ZHCET Chapter conducted three major student-oriented activities spanning technical exposure, competitive engagement, and collaborative event organisation. Across these events, the chapter maintained active student participation, expanded its visibility within the student community, and contributed to the development of technical awareness, problem-solving aptitude, and presentation confidence among participants.

The activities organised during this session reflected growth in chapter operations as well as in student outreach. Collectively, these events helped strengthen chapter activity, encouraged engagement across different technical domains, and promoted a competitive and collaborative learning environment within the institution.

## Detailed Event Notes

### 1. Ignite Hackathon
**Date:** 11-12 October 2025  
**Type:** Hackathon conducted in collaboration with STARlab Capital

**Objective:**  
The event was designed to provide students with an opportunity to participate in a structured hackathon environment, with the added motivation of potential internship opportunities associated with the collaboration.

**Audience:**  
The hackathon was targeted primarily at 3rd and 4th year students, who were better positioned to engage with the applied problem-solving format of the event.

**Execution Format:**  
Ignite Hackathon was planned as a two-round event. The first round was successfully conducted, during which student teams were given two days to work on assigned problem statements and prepare solutions. At the conclusion of this stage, teams presented their ideas and solutions before invited judges. The second round could not be conducted due to institutional process constraints.

**Club Contribution:**  
The CodeChef ZHCET Chapter undertook the organisation and publicity of the event, coordinated with invited judges, and managed the formal communication process with the collaborating company. The editorial team also contributed to official correspondence, discussion of event details, and coordination regarding prizes and the proposed internship-linked structure.

**Student Outcome and Impact:**  
The event gave participants practical exposure to the structure and expectations of a hackathon, including solution development under time constraints and presentation before a judging panel. Despite the event not progressing beyond the first round, it still served as a valuable learning experience for students and contributed to the chapter's growth in external coordination and event execution.

### 2. TeckTrek
**Date:** November 2025  
**Type:** Workshop

**Objective:**  
TeckTrek was organised to introduce early-year students to a range of important technical domains and help them build initial awareness of the opportunities available in different fields of computing.

**Audience:**  
The workshop was conducted mainly for 1st year students, with a focus on helping them understand possible technical pathways at an early stage of their academic journey.

**Execution Format:**  
The workshop followed an orientation-style format and covered major areas such as Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, Web Development, Competitive Programming, and Git with Version Control. The sessions were delivered by members of the club itself, allowing participants to receive peer-led guidance from students already active in these domains. A registration fee of Rs. 100 was kept for participation, and certificates were provided to attendees.

**Club Contribution:**  
The chapter was responsible for organising the workshop, managing publicity and editorial coordination, and delivering the sessions through internal club speakers. This event demonstrated the chapter's capacity to mobilise its own members as knowledge-sharing contributors.

**Student Outcome and Impact:**  
TeckTrek gave students an introductory understanding of several technical fields and helped them gain early insight into the skills, tools, and learning directions associated with each area. The event received a positive response and contributed to the continued growth of chapter activity and student engagement.

### 3. Code Heat
**Date:** March 2026  
**Type:** Coding Contest on HackerRank

**Objective:**  
The contest was organised to foster competitive coding practice, strengthen problem-solving ability, and create an engaging environment for students interested in programming challenges.

**Audience:**  
The event was open to students from all academic years, enabling broad participation across batches.

**Execution Format:**  
Code Heat was conducted on the HackerRank platform. The contest was created and structured on HackerRank by the Editorial Lead, and participants competed in a timed coding environment designed to encourage accuracy, speed, and logical thinking. Cash prizes were awarded to the top three performers: Rs. 500 for first place, Rs. 300 for second place, and Rs. 200 for third place.

**Club Contribution:**  
The CodeChef ZHCET Chapter handled organisation, publicity, editorial coordination, judging support, and overall contest management. The event reflected the chapter's active role in developing and delivering competitive programming opportunities for the student community.

**Student Outcome and Impact:**  
Code Heat created a competitive yet constructive environment for students to test their programming skills. It encouraged problem-solving under contest conditions and further strengthened the culture of coding participation within the chapter.
