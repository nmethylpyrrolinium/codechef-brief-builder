Session Overview

During the Academic Year 2024-25, the CodeChef ZHCET Chapter organised a series of technical workshops, collaborative learning sessions, and coding events aimed at increasing student engagement in programming and emerging technologies. The chapter's work during this session focused on practical skill-building, peer learning, and exposing students to multiple technical domains including web development, app development, artificial intelligence, and competitive coding.

Even where detailed participation records are not currently available, the session clearly reflects active chapter engagement through a variety of events conducted in collaboration with student communities and external industry contributors. These activities helped strengthen the chapter's presence and encouraged students to explore technical learning opportunities beyond the classroom.

Detailed Event Notes

1. Unlocking the Power of Web: HTML, CSS & JavaScript

Date: 5-6 October 2024

Type: Two-day workshop in collaboration with Polytechnic Roboclub

Venue: TPO Auditorium, ZHCET

Objective: This workshop was organised to introduce students to the fundamentals of web development and help them begin their journey in building websites and interactive web applications.

Brief Description: The programme was conducted over two days. The first day focused on HTML and CSS basics, including webpage structure and visual presentation. The second day introduced JavaScript concepts along with API integration and deployment-oriented exposure. The event served as an entry-level hands-on learning opportunity for students interested in web technologies.

Impact: The workshop helped students gain early familiarity with essential web development concepts and reflected the chapter's efforts to promote practical technical learning through collaborative initiatives.

2. CodeHeat November

Date: 6 November 2024

Type: Coding Contest

Venue: TPO Auditorium

Objective: CodeHeat November was organised to promote problem-solving, coding practice, and a healthy competitive environment among students interested in programming.

Brief Description: The event was presented as a chapter coding challenge with cash prizes for top performers. It encouraged participants to test their coding skills under time constraints and engage in a focused contest environment.

Impact: This event contributed to strengthening competitive coding culture within the student community and highlighted the chapter's role in encouraging regular coding engagement.

3. Atalgo Engineering Masterclass

Date: 19 December 2024

Type: Industry Masterclass in collaboration with Atalgo Engineering

Mode: Online

Objective: This session was organised to provide students with industry exposure in the fields of Generative AI, enterprise AI systems, and software testing while connecting technical learning with career and internship awareness.

Brief Description: The masterclass introduced students to Atalgo Engineering's enterprise AI platform, Flame, and discussed how Generative AI can support complex workflows and automation-oriented processes. The session also linked these ideas to skill development, industry applications, and professional opportunities.

Impact: The event broadened student awareness of current AI trends and demonstrated the chapter's effort to bring industry-oriented technical exposure to the campus community.

4. App Development Workshop

Date: 31 January-2 February 2025

Type: App Development Workshop in collaboration with GDGC ZHCET

Venue: TPO Auditorium

Objective: The workshop was conducted to introduce students to app development concepts and help them begin exploring mobile application development using Flutter.

Brief Description: This was presented as a multi-day workshop focused on app development learning. The event encouraged students to bring app ideas to life and provided an introductory pathway into Flutter-based application building through a collaborative chapter event.

Impact: The workshop expanded the chapter's technical outreach beyond web and competitive coding by including app development as another practical learning avenue for students.
